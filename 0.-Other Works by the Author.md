Other Works by the Author

The world functions because it has limits.
But what happens when they disappear?
What begins as progress can turn into a threat. As time accumulates without end, decisions lose weight, systems become saturated, and balance begins to erode.
These works explore, from different perspectives, the same fundamental question: what happens when the limits that sustain reality cease to exist?

IRAN: The System That Challenges the World
Power, Strategy, and Global Influence
What if everything you think you know about Iran is wrong?
For decades, Iran has been analyzed through headlines—crises, threats, negotiations, conflicts. Yet despite the constant attention, one reality remains:
Iran is still misunderstood.
Because Iran is not just a country.
It is a system.
IRAN: The System That Challenges the World breaks away from conventional analysis and exposes the deeper structure behind one of the most complex actors in global politics.
This is not a timeline of events.
This is a decoding.
Inside, you will discover:
•	Why traditional geopolitical frameworks fail to explain Iran 
•	How a system can survive revolutions, sanctions, and global isolation 
•	The internal logic that drives Iran’s strategic decisions 
•	Why pressure does not produce the expected outcomes 
•	How Iran projects influence far beyond its borders 
This book challenges the way Iran is perceived—and replaces assumptions with structure.
Clear. Precise. Uncomfortable.
If you want to understand what Iran does, you can follow the news.
If you want to understand why it does it, you need to understand the system.
This book gives you that system.
